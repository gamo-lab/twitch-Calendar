Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year, also known as the Spring Festival, is a festival that marks the beginning of a new year on the traditional lunisolar Chinese calendar. It is one of the most important holidays in Chinese culture. It has been added to the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity list by the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organisation in 2024. Marking the end of winter and the beginning of spring, this festival takes place from Chinese New Year's Eve (the evening preceding the first day of the year) to the Lantern Festival, held on the 15th day of the year. The first day of the Chinese New Year falls on the new moon that appears between 21 January and 20 February.
